UX Designer
2020 – Present
TXOne Networks · B2B OT Cybersecurity · Taipei

As the sole UX designer, I own the full design process — from problem definition and wireframing through cross-functional negotiation with PM, RD, and JM, to final delivery. My work spans two parallel tracks:

  • PM-driven product developmentWork closely with PMs to understand business goals and core feature value, then translate that into design proposals.
  • VIP client sessionsAttend weekly client meetings to directly interview and clarify their root pain points, then propose solutions that address VIP needs while remaining applicable to the broader global user base.

Here are some of the key contributions and practices that define my work at TXOne:

  • Led end-to-end UX design across a suite of industrial cybersecurity products
    From portable scanning devices (Portable Inspector) and kiosk-based entry control stations (Safe Port) to a centralized management platform (ElementOne) handling asset inventory, scan logs, and vulnerability tracking.
  • Proposed and compared alternative design solutions under technical constraints
    When RD raised timeline concerns, independently generated multiple approaches presented in Figma with clear trade-offs and scope implications, enabling PM to make informed prioritization decisions.
  • User Research & Field Observation
    Conducted user research through a combination of regular remote interviews and occasional on-site factory visits, observing real operational environments and translating findings into design directions.
  • Initiated pre-launch usability validation
    Arranged real client walkthrough before product release, uncovering multiple usability issues simultaneously and implementing corrections prior to go-live.
  • Built and maintained Design System from scratch
    Established component structures, naming conventions, and usage guidelines that reduced design-to-development friction and improved delivery consistency.
